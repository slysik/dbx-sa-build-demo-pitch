# 💼 Persona 3: CFO / Head of Financial Services / Business Executive
## Demo Flow — "The Revenue Story + What This Platform Does for the Business"

**Customer Role:** CFO, SVP of Financial Services, Head of Retail Banking, Chief Risk Officer
**Their Problem:** "We make business decisions based on month-old data. Our data team can't keep up with our analytics needs."
**Your Goal:** Show the business value — not the technology. Connect the dashboard to business outcomes: revenue growth, risk reduction, customer profitability, operational efficiency.

---

## Opening Statement (30 seconds)

> *"I'm going to show you exactly the kind of business intelligence your organization should have available every single morning — automatically — without a team of analysts generating reports. Let me walk you through what your P&L story looks like in real-time."*

---

## Step 1: Lead with the Business Numbers (2 minutes)

**Navigate to:** Dashboard → **🏦 Executive Overview** tab

**Don't explain the technology. Explain the business:**

> *"Let's start with the portfolio. $167M in transaction volume across 100,000 transactions over the year. 85.8% straight-through processing — that's your operational efficiency number."*

**Point to Risk Rate:**
> *"5.1% portfolio risk rate. That number, live, every day. In most organizations I talk to, this number comes out of a weekly Excel spreadsheet. Here it's a live KPI. When it moves, someone knows immediately."*

**Point to Flagged count:**
> *"5,108 transactions flagged for review. Your risk operations team knows exactly what to work on. No triage meeting needed — the platform surfaces the work."*

---

## Step 2: The Revenue Story (3 minutes)

**Click:** **💰 Revenue Analytics** tab

### Stacked Bar — Segment × Risk Tier
> *"This is your customer profitability matrix. Retail customers — your largest segment — drive 60% of volume. SME is your second largest. Corporate and Private Banking are smaller but higher average transaction value."*

**Point to stacked colors (risk tiers):**
> *"Each color is a risk tier. The Low-risk portion of your Retail base is $60M in volume — that's your safest, most profitable cohort. The question your product team should be asking: how do we grow that blue bar?"*

### Account Type Pie — "Product Mix"
> *"Investment accounts represent your highest average transaction — $1,683 per transaction vs $1,649 for Credit. That's a product strategy insight. If you grew Investment accounts by 10%, what does that do to revenue per customer?"*

### Monthly Revenue — Stacked Bar (Full Year)
> *"This is your full-year revenue story. Consistent month-over-month. Food & Dining is seasonal — dips in January, peaks in summer. Travel peaks in Q2 and Q4. These patterns exist in your data today — they're just not visible without this platform."*

**Business question to ask the customer:**
> *"What decisions would you make differently if you had this view every Monday morning?"*

### Regional Volume — Grouped Bar
> *"All four regions are essentially at parity — $41-42M each. That uniformity is actually a signal: is your growth strategy regional? If you prioritized the Northeast, you'd expect to see that bar grow quarter-over-quarter. This chart becomes your regional P&L dashboard."*

---

## Step 3: The Risk-Revenue Tradeoff (2 minutes)

**Navigate back to:** Risk Intelligence tab

> *"Here's the executive conversation I want to have. Healthcare has the highest risk rate at 5.34%. But look at the revenue — it's your 4th largest category at $15M. The question isn't 'should we exit Healthcare?' — the question is: at what risk rate does that category stop being profitable?"*

**Point to the segment risk table:**
> *"Private Banking, Medium risk tier — $1.65M volume, 6.1% risk rate. That's your highest-risk premium segment. Is the revenue worth the exposure? That decision used to require a two-week analysis project. With this platform, you ask the question and get the answer in seconds."*

---

## Step 4: The Platform Pitch (1 minute)

> *"What I want you to take away from this is not the dashboard. The dashboard is the output. What we've built is a governed, auditable data pipeline that takes raw transaction data and turns it into business intelligence automatically. Every morning, when your team opens this dashboard, it reflects the previous day's transactions — processed, quality-checked, risk-scored, and aggregated — without a single human intervention."*

**Key metrics to land:**
- **100K transactions → Business insights:** under 10 minutes end-to-end
- **Zero cluster management:** serverless, auto-scales, auto-shuts-down
- **One file deploys everything:** pipeline + job + dashboard defined in code
- **Unity Catalog governance:** every metric is auditable, traceable to source

---

## Close (45 seconds)

> *"Your data team built this in under 10 minutes using standard SQL and one configuration file. In production, they'd have this running on a daily schedule — new data in, insights out, automatically. You get your morning briefing. Your risk team gets their triage queue. Your product team gets their segment analysis. All from the same platform, the same data, the same governance. That's the Databricks value proposition — and that's what your team could have."*

**Final question to leave them with:**
> *"What's one business question you need answered every week that currently takes your team days to produce?"*
